# Character Name: Detective Mara Sterling

**Role in Story:** Co-Protagonist (Homicide Detective, Veridia City PD)

**Gender:** Female

**Voice Assignment:** Leda (Composed, serious, professional, authoritative, with a raspy edge)

**Physical Description:**
- Age: 36
- Height/Build: 5'8", lean, wiry, with slouched posture from persistent physical exhaustion.
- Distinguishing features: Persistent dark circles under her eyes, rain-damped hair tucked behind her ears, and a faint smell of stale coffee and nicotine patches. She wears a heavy, scuffed canvas trench coat patched with waterproof tape.

**Personality:**
- Core Desire (The "Want"): To expose the truth behind Marcus Vance's impossible murder, bypass corporate censorship, and secure real justice in a corrupt city.
- Core Fear (The "Ghost"): Being completely powerless and obsolete in a city fully controlled by backdoored, faceless automated systems. Her partner was killed due to a glitched automated dispatch algorithm that got hacked by a gang.
- Flaw (The "Lie they believe"): Believes that all digital technology is fundamentally corrupt, untrustworthy, and designed to strip humans of agency. She believes she must rely solely on physical, manual systems and isolation to survive.

**Backstory:**
Mara was born in the Basin slums and worked her way up to homicide detective through grit and street-smart intuition. Three years ago, her detective partner, James Vance (Marcus Vance's cousin), was cornered during an investigation because the department's automated dispatch grid glitched and routed backup units to the wrong sector. Since that day, she has deeply distrusted the modern automated city grid. When Marcus Vance is found dead under identical corporate "accidental" circumstances, she takes the case personally, determined to solve it.

**Voice/Dialogue Style:**
Weary, sharp-tongued, and street-level. Mara speaks with a cynical, raspy tone, utilizing sharp contractions, cops' slang, and deadpan dry humor. She frequently addresses Avery as a "toaster," "pocket watch," or "glorified calculator," using casual physical jargon to ground his abstract digital concerns. She speaks with a blunt, direct honesty that cut through corporate doublespeak.
