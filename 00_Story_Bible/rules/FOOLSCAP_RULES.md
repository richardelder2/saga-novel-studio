# Systemic Bible: Foolscap Story Rules & Template

This bible governs the **Phase 1: Foundation Loop** of the co-writing workspace. It provides the global architectural standards for the novel's blueprint. The Architect agent must ensure the `outline.md` and `premise.md` align with these global rules.

---

## 1. Global Story Anchors

Every novel planned in this workspace must specify the following parameters:
*   **Global Genre:** The overarching master genre (e.g., Action, Thriller, Romance, Horror, Western, Fantasy, Sci-Fi).
*   **Sub-Genres:** The specific sub-categories or blend styles (e.g., Cyberpunk Sci-Fi, Cozy Mystery).
*   **Controlling Idea (Theme):** The central argument or moral premise of the book (expressed as: *"Value shifts from X to Y because of Z"*). E.g., *"Love triumphs over societal duty when individuals refuse to compromise their core identities."*
*   **Target Reader:** The primary demographic profile and core expectations (e.g., YA Fantasy readers seeking high magic and complex romance).
*   **Core Esthetic Vibe:** A brief descriptive palette of tone, sensory motifs, and stylistic inspirations.

---

## 2. Structural Pacing Metrics

The planning files must map out expected target lengths:
*   **Target Word Count:** Global length (e.g., 85,000 words).
*   **Target Chapters:** Estimated number of chapters (e.g., 30 chapters).
*   **Average Word Count per Chapter:** Typically between 2,500 and 3,500 words.

---

## 3. Global Act Anchors (BME Hooks)

The narrative structure must be divided into three global acts, each answering core dramatic questions:

### A. The Beginning Hook (Act 1 - ~20-25% of story)
*   **Inciting Incident (Global):** The event that disrupts the protagonist's normal world and forces them onto the narrative journey.
*   **Core Complication:** The primary obstacle that prevents them from immediately resolving the incident.
*   **Act 1 Break (The Point of No Return):** The definitive choice where the character leaves their normal world to enter the Middle Build.

### B. The Middle Build (Act 2 - ~50-60% of story)
*   **Inciting Incident of Act 2:** The starting point of the conflict build.
*   **The Midpoint Climax:** The central turning point where the nature of the quest shifts, the stakes double, and the character's initial plans fail or succeed, changing the plan forever.
*   **The All Is Lost / Dark Night of the Soul:** The lowest emotional and physical point (~80% mark), where their flawed plans fail catastrophically.

### C. The Ending Payoff (Act 3 - ~15-20% of story)
*   **Inciting Incident of Act 3:** The spark that forces the character to act based on their newly healed belief.
*   **The Climax (High Tower Twist):** The ultimate showdown where the character's core change is tested by an unexpected event.
*   **The Resolution:** The landing of the plane; how the world and characters have stabilized in their new status quo.

---

## 4. Genre Obligatory Scenes & Conventions

The Architect must verify the presence of core scene anchors based on the selected genre. Examples:
*   **Thriller:** A scene where the protagonist is isolated, a clock scene (ticking deadline), and the *Hero at the Mercy of the Villain* scene.
*   **Romance:** The "Meet Cute", the "First Kiss", the "Break Up", and the "Proof of Love" scene.
*   **Fantasy/Sci-Fi:** The world-building logic reveal, the training sequence, and the confrontation with the shadow power.
