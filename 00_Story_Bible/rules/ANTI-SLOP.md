# Systemic Bible: Anti-Slop & Prose Guidelines

This bible governs the **Phase 2: Drafting Loop** and **Phase 3: Editorial Loop** of the co-writing workspace. It provides structural and stylistic rules to eliminate typical AI-generated clichés, filter words, and generic prose, ensuring the manuscript reads like premium, professional fiction.

---

## 1. Cliché & Slop Word Blacklist

The Scribe and Deep Editor agents must actively scan and purge the following phrases and words:

### Narrative Clichés
*   **Avoid:** *"A testament to..."*, *"A dance of..."*, *"A beacon of hope..."*, *"Beacon of light..."*, *"In that moment..."*
*   **Avoid:** *"With a newfound sense of..."*, *"Little did they know..."*, *"A cold dread washed over..."*, *"Heart skipped a beat..."*, *"Shiver down their spine..."*
*   **Avoid:** *"Only time would tell..."*, *"The air was thick with..."*, *"Whispered in hushed tones..."*

### Overused Action Beats
*   **Avoid:** *"He smirked..."*, *"She scoffed..."*, *"They gasped..."*, *"He let out a breath he didn't know he was holding..."* (Replace with subtle physical reactions or direct sensory descriptions).

---

## 2. Eliminate Filter Words (POV Filtering)

Filter words distance the reader from the protagonist's direct sensory experience. Agents must draft using direct immersion:

| POV Filtered (Avoid) | Deep Immersion (Use Instead) |
| :--- | :--- |
| She *heard* the floorboards creak behind her. | The floorboards creaked behind her. |
| He *noticed* that the lights were flickering. | The lights flickered. |
| She *felt* a sudden wave of panic. | Panic tightened her throat. |
| He *saw* the massive wolf standing in the clearing. | The massive wolf stood in the clearing. |
| She *realized* that she had been betrayed. | The pieces clicked together—she was betrayed. |

---

## 3. High-Quality Dialogue Guidelines

AI dialogue tends to be overly formal, exposition-heavy, and balanced. Ensure:
*   **Contractions:** Characters must use contractions (e.g., *"I'm"*, *"don't"*, *"won't"*) unless their character profile specifies a highly formal or robotic speaking style.
*   **No Exposition Dumping:** Characters must not explain things to each other that they both already know just to inform the reader (*"As you know, Bob..."*).
*   **Subtext:** Dialogue should have subtext. Characters rarely say exactly what they are thinking or feeling; they speak around their desires.
*   **Diverse Sentence Structure:** Vary dialogue lengths and avoid ending every line with a generic beat (e.g. *"she said with a smile"*).

---

## 4. Scene-Level Prose Rules

*   **No "Bedtime" Endings:** Avoid ending chapters with characters simply going to sleep, reflecting on the day, or drifting into unconsciousness, unless it is a vital plot complication.
*   **Active Verbs:** Use strong, active verbs instead of passive constructions (e.g. *"He sprinted"* instead of *"He was starting to run"*).
*   **Specific Nouns:** Use concrete, specific details (e.g., *"a beat-up 1998 Honda Civic"* instead of *"a rusty car"*).
