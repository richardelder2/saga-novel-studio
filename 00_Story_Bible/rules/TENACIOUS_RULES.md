# Systemic Bible: Tenacious Writing Rules & Character Arcs

This bible governs the **Phase 1: Foundation Loop** and the **Phase 2: Drafting Loop** of the co-writing workspace. It ensures that the plot events of the outline are not arbitrary, but are the direct, inevitable consequences of the protagonist's flawed choices and internal evolution.

---

## 1. The Internal Story Foundations

Before any plotting is allowed, the Architect and Scribe must verify that the protagonist has a clearly defined internal engine:

*   **Internal Obstacle (Flawed Belief):** The SPECIFIC flawed worldview, perspective, or deep-seated lie the character believes when the story starts. It harms them, their relationships, or their world, and it MUST change.
*   **Story Point (Healed Belief):** The thematic message of the novel. By the end of a positive change arc, the character adopts this healed belief. It is the lesson you, as the author, want to share with the reader.
*   **External Goal:** The tangible, all-consuming plot target that the character actively pursues throughout Act 2 (e.g., win the competition, escape the prison, find the relic).
*   **Internal Goal:** The subconscious, hidden desire of the character's heart (safety, connection, respect, stability, belonging).

---

## 2. The Internal Logic Proof

Every story plan must satisfy the **Internal Logic Proof** to ensure character motivations are tightly integrated:

```text
The main character wants to [External Goal].
They believe that if they can get [External Goal], then they will also finally get [Internal Goal].
They ONLY believe their [External Goal] will give them their [Internal Goal] because they hold the flawed belief of [Internal Obstacle].
```

**Rule for Agents:** If an outline fails this logic proof, the *Evaluator* agent must flag it as structurally disjointed.

---

## 3. The 6-Beat Character Arc of Change

The core plot beats of the novel must mirror the character's psychological arc:

1.  **Internal Obstacle Status Quo (1-10%):** We see the character operating under their flawed belief in their normal world.
2.  **Catalyst Plot Event (10-12%):** An external problem happens TO the character. It changes their status quo and actively triggers their flawed belief, forcing them to react.
3.  **Act 2 Break "Flawed" Plan (20-25%):** The character enacts a plan to achieve their goals. **Crucial Rule:** This plan must be a flawed plan of action, completely driven and rationalized by their *Internal Obstacle* flawed belief.
4.  **All Is Lost Plot Failure (~80%):** A massive plot catastrophe occurs. **Crucial Rule:** This failure must be the character's fault—the direct consequence of their flawed Act 2 Break Plan of action.
5.  **Dark Night Lesson (~80-85%):** Stripped of their plan, the character wrestles with the consequences of their flawed belief. They must either choose to learn the healed *Story Point* lesson (positive arc) or reject it (negative arc).
6.  **Act 3 Climax Action (85-100%):** The character enacts a *Final Pivot Plan* driven by their healed *Story Point* belief. Their new action resolves the plot climax, leading to success.
