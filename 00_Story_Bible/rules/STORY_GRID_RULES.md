# Systemic Bible: Story Grid Scene Rules

This bible governs the **Phase 2: Drafting Loop** and **Phase 3: Editorial Loop** of the co-writing workspace. It provides structural scene-level quality control. The Scribe and Deep Editor agents must verify that every drafted scene satisfies these guidelines.

---

## 1. The 5 Commandments of a Scene

Every scene or chapter drafted must have a clear beginning, middle, and end, defined by these five structural checkpoints:

1.  **Inciting Incident:** An event (either *Causal*—caused by a character's choice, or *Coincidental*—happening by chance) that throws the characters in the scene out of emotional or physical balance. It sets a scene-level goal.
2.  **Progressive Complications:** A series of obstacles, conflicts, or escalating issues that prevent the character from achieving their scene goal. It builds tension and leads to a **Turning Point** (where a new piece of information or an action changes the stakes entirely).
3.  **Crisis:** The central question of the scene. The protagonist faces a difficult decision with no easy answer. It must be either:
    *   *The Best Bad Choice:* A choice between two negative outcomes.
    *   *Irreconcilable Goods:* A choice between two positive outcomes that are mutually exclusive.
4.  **Climax:** The active choice and execution of that choice by the protagonist. They DO something in response to the Crisis.
5.  **Resolution:** The immediate, real-world consequence of the Climax choice. The scene lands in a new state of stability.

---

## 2. The Value Shift

A scene is not a scene unless a core **Life Value** shifts from the beginning to the end. Life values represent human conditions:
*   **Life vs. Death** (Alive $\rightarrow$ Injured $\rightarrow$ Dying $\rightarrow$ Dead)
*   **Safety vs. Danger** (Safe $\rightarrow$ Threatened $\rightarrow$ Endangered $\rightarrow$ Captured)
*   **Love vs. Hate** (Attraction $\rightarrow$ Connection $\rightarrow$ Conflict $\rightarrow$ Betrayal $\rightarrow$ Hate)
*   **Knowledge vs. Ignorance** (Ignorance $\rightarrow$ Clue $\rightarrow$ Revelation $\rightarrow$ Wisdom)
*   **Success vs. Failure** (Hopeful $\rightarrow$ Blocked $\rightarrow$ Failed $\rightarrow$ Defeated)

**Rule for Scribe & Editor:** If a drafted scene has the same value at the beginning and the end (e.g. starts with safe characters talking, and they are still safe with no new information at the end), it is **exposition, not a scene**. It must be rejected or rewritten.

---

## 3. Scene Polarity

Every scene must track a charge shift:
*   **Positive to Negative (+ $\rightarrow$ -):** The scene starts well but complications cause a failure or crisis, ending in a negative state.
*   **Negative to Positive (- $\rightarrow$ +):** The scene starts in conflict or danger but the character's climax resolves the crisis, ending in a positive state.

The *Evaluator* agent must review drafted chapters and document:
*   *Starting Value & Polarity*
*   *Ending Value & Polarity*
*   *The Value Shift Delta*
