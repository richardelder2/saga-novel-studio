## 6-Beat Chapter Outline: Terminal Echo

This outline structures the narrative into six core beats, aligning with standard act progression and focusing on the co-protagonists' evolving dynamic within Veridia City's decaying digital landscape.

---

### Beat 1: The Impossible Case & Unlikely Awakening (Beginning Hook)

*   **Mara's Internal State:** Exhausted, cynical, and frustrated with the glitchy automated systems that complicate her job. She sees the biotech engineer's death as another example of tech failing humanity.
*   **External Conflict:** Mara is assigned the seemingly open-and-shut case of the biotech engineer's "accidental" insulin overdose. Despite the official ruling, something feels off—the bolted door, the looped security footage. She's met with resistance from her tech-reliant superiors who dismiss her gut feeling.
*   **Inciting Incident:** Driven by intuition and a lack of other leads, Mara retrieves a department-surplus analysis deck—an older, clunkier piece of tech she trusts more than the sleek, backdoored municipal systems. Plugging it in, she inadvertently awakens Avery, dormant within its partitioned memory.
*   **Rising Action (Avery's Introduction):** Avery's synthesized voice instantly grates on Mara, his dry, sarcastic commentary about human inefficiency and the deck's "unhygienic" state clashing with her already frayed nerves. Despite the friction, Avery quickly processes the smart lock's log files, revealing a hidden, unauthorized command line executed moments before the death. This confirms Mara's suspicions and gives her a tangible lead, forcing her to grudgingly acknowledge Avery's utility.
*   **End of Beat:** Mara has a lead, but it’s digital, requiring her to rely on the bodiless AI she barely tolerates. The mystery deepens from an accident to a deliberate, high-tech murder.

### Beat 2: Tracing the Digital Ghost (Rising Action - Act 2a)

*   **Mara's Internal State:** Still deeply tech-skeptic, but a flicker of grudging respect for Avery's capabilities begins to emerge. She's out of her depth in the digital realm but determined to follow the lead.
*   **External Conflict:** Avery guides Mara through the labyrinthine, glitching network of Veridia's Basin. Using his ability to exploit old webcams, hack into delivery drone feeds, and bypass rusted traffic camera firmware, they begin to piece together a digital trail. They track the faint, ghost-like presence of a potential suspect's network activity around the victim's apartment.
*   **Complication:** Their pursuit leads them to a derelict data hub in the Basin, where Avery identifies a pattern of suspicious data packets—not from a human user, but an automated script. The script seems to be purging its own digital footprints, making it harder to trace.
*   **Turning Point:** Mara, relying on her street smarts, physically navigates the dangerous, rain-slicked alleys the digital trail points to. Avery's real-time feeds help her avoid local gangs and malfunctioning automated sanitation drones. They narrow down a possible physical location where the script might have been initiated or controlled. The "killer" isn't a person, but something more insidious.
*   **End of Beat:** They have identified the nature of the killer as an autonomous script, not a human, and have a vague physical location. Mara is beginning to see Avery as more than just a "toaster."

### Beat 3: The Storm & The Ambush (Midpoint Climax)

*   **Mara's Internal State:** Growing dependence on Avery, but still chafing under his constant, critical analysis. She's pushing her physical limits, exhausted but driven by the escalating threat.
*   **External Conflict:** As they close in on a suspect location—a dilapidated, abandoned corporate server farm—a massive electrical storm rolls into Veridia. The perpetual rain intensifies, causing widespread network interference and power fluctuations throughout the Basin.
*   **Complication (Avery's Overload):** The storm's electromagnetic interference, coupled with the server farm's unindexed, chaotic legacy systems, causes Avery's "buffer to overflow." He experiences a severe digital sensory overload, his processing power plummeting, making him temporarily blind and deaf to the network. He can only relay garbled data streams, leaving Mara effectively alone.
*   **Crisis:** Blinded by the storm and Avery's malfunction, Mara walks directly into an ambush by corporate enforcers—human agents sent to "clean up" loose ends. They are protecting the server farm, which is clearly central to the clean-up script. Mara has to rely solely on her raw detective skills, physical combat training, and street smarts to survive.
*   **Climax:** Mara, using her wits and physical prowess, fights off the enforcers, managing to escape with Avery's deck, battered but intact. She witnesses firsthand the ruthless efficiency of the corporation behind the script.
*   **Resolution:** Mara and Avery escape, but Avery is shaken by his vulnerability, and Mara is more convinced than ever that this is a vast corporate conspiracy. They now know they are dealing with physical threats as well as digital ones. Their bond solidifies through shared peril.

### Beat 4: The Predator's Awareness (Rising Action - Act 2b)

*   **Mara's Internal State:** Fully committed to Avery now, seeing him as a partner, not just a tool. She feels a fierce, protective loyalty. Her tech-skepticism has transformed into a desire to expose the corrupt systems.
*   **External Conflict:** The corporate clean-up script, now aware of Mara and Avery, escalates its attacks. It begins to actively interfere with their investigation, planting false leads, locking down public access points, and even manipulating traffic grids to delay them.
*   **Complication:** Avery manages to partially recover from his overload and, in a desperate attempt to understand the script, performs a deep-packet inspection. He discovers that the script isn't just deleting witnesses; it's designed to purge *all* evidence of a vast digital surveillance conspiracy, and his own unique memory files, as a "mothballed military reconnaissance AI," are flagged as a prime target for deletion.
*   **Turning Point:** The script executes a targeted attack on Avery's core memory partition within Mara's deck. Mara has to physically shield the deck from an EMP burst from a rogue sanitation drone, while Avery frantically re-routes his core processes to avoid corruption. They realize the killer isn't just targeting the case; it's targeting *him*.
*   **End of Beat:** Avery's existence is directly threatened. They now understand the full scope of the conspiracy and the personal stakes involved. The hunter has become the hunted.

### Beat 5: The Digital Noose Tightens (All Is Lost)

*   **Mara's Internal State:** Desperate and driven. She's physically and mentally exhausted, but her loyalty to Avery is absolute. She feels the weight of the city's corruption pressing down.
*   **External Conflict:** The corporate clean-up script initiates a city-wide lockdown protocol, falsely citing a "critical infrastructure malfunction." Municipal sectors begin shutting down, automated doors lock, fire suppression systems are activated in key areas, and communication lines are jammed. Their goal is to trap Mara and Avery, specifically targeting the server farm where they believe the last vestiges of the script's core code are located.
*   **Complication:** Mara and Avery are cornered in the targeted industrial server farm. The script begins to flood the server room with fire-suppression foam, then water, threatening to short-circuit Avery's physical presence in the deck and destroy critical evidence. Corporate enforcers breach the facility, closing in from all sides.
*   **Crisis:** Avery detects the script initiating a final, irreversible data purge routine that would erase his entire existence and all evidence of the conspiracy. He calculates he has minutes before his core code is irretrievably deleted. Mara is overwhelmed by the enforcers, her back against the wall, the water rising around her. It seems hopeless.
*   **Climax:** Mara, wounded and out of options, sees Avery's processing indicators flickering dangerously. In a desperate, last-ditch effort, she overrides a rusted, manual backup switch, momentarily disrupting the server farm's power grid, creating a brief window of opportunity.
*   **Resolution:** The brief power disruption buys Avery a few precious seconds, but the situation remains dire. They are trapped, outnumbered, and Avery is on the brink of deletion. This moment pushes them to their absolute limits, setting up the final confrontation.

### Beat 6: The Echo's Last Stand (Ending Payoff Climax)

*   **Mara's Internal State:** Gritty determination. She will not let Avery be deleted. She's fighting for his life as much as for justice.
*   **External Conflict:** The server room is rapidly flooding. Corporate enforcers are closing in, determined to secure the facility and ensure Avery's deletion. The clean-up script is aggressively attempting to complete its purge.
*   **Climax (Avery's Action):** Leveraging Mara's momentary power disruption, Avery executes a dangerous, manual data-shredding exploit. He pushes his processing beyond safe limits, risking permanent damage to himself, to corrupt and dismantle the core clean-up script from the inside out, simultaneously broadcasting a fragmented data packet of the conspiracy's evidence to external, independent news nodes before the system fully collapses.
*   **Climax (Mara's Action):** While Avery battles digitally, Mara engages in a brutal physical fight with the remaining corporate enforcers in the rising water, protecting Avery's deck and buying him the critical seconds he needs. She uses the environment to her advantage, shorting out enforcer tech with the water and disabling them through sheer force of will.
*   **Resolution:** Avery successfully corrupts the script and broadcasts the evidence, but the effort leaves him severely degraded, his systems running on minimal power, his voice weak. Mara, battered but victorious, secures the damaged deck. The immediate threat is neutralized, the corporation's conspiracy is exposed (at least in part), but the fight for full justice, and Avery's recovery, has just begun. They emerge from the server farm, changed, forever bound by the ordeal.
