# Tenacious Plotting Workbook

Based on the *Novel Plotting Untangled* method.

## Part 1: internal Logic Proof

**Character Name:** [Name]

-   **External Goal:** (What tangible thing do they want?)
-   **Internal Goal:** (What emotional need are they trying to fill? e.g., respect, safety)
-   **Internal Obstacle (Flawed Belief):** (What lie do they believe about the world?)
-   **Story Point (Healed Belief):** (What truth must they learn?)

**The Proof:**
> [Character] wants **[External Goal]**.
> They believe that if they get it, they will finally get **[Internal Goal]**.
> They ONLY believe this because they believe **[Internal Obstacle]**.

---

## Part 2: The Pivot Plans

Map out how their plan changes as the story progresses.

### Act 1: The Status Quo
-   **Current Belief:** Flawed.
-   **Initial Plan:** [What are they doing when we meet them?]

### Act 2a: The First Pivot (The Reaction)
*Triggered by the Catalyst.*
-   **The Plan:** [How do they plan to get their goal using their OLD methods/beliefs?]
-   **Why it fails:** [How does the plot punish this flawed belief?]

### Act 2b: The Second Pivot (The Midpoint Shift)
*Triggered by the Midpoint realization that the old plan is impossible.*
-   **The Plan:** [They try harder, or try a new angle, but are still clinging to the Flawed Belief.]
-   **The All Is Lost:** [The ultimate failure of this plan.]

### Act 3: The Final Pivot (The Resolution)
*Triggered by the Dark Night Lesson.*
-   **The Plan:** [Now driven by the **Story Point/Healed Belief**.]
-   **The Result:** [Success (or tragic failure if they didn't change).]
